Poll Results

All in all the over-all votes are broken down according to how each region voted, replicating the turn out of last elections.
The APC are leading on 57%, PDP on 40% while other political parties are on 3%.

Looking at employments status and how people voted across parties, voters with no employments voted 55% for APC, 42% PDP and others 3%.On the other hand voters who are employed voted 59% APC ,37%PDP and others parties received 3%.

Female voters, voted 61% for APC, PDP got 35%,while other political parties got 4% vote. The male counter-part voted 56% for APC, 41% for PDP and 3% for other political parties.

Voters with income 20-30k per month voted 10% for APC, 7% for PDP and no vote for others parties, voters with income 30-40k per month voted 5% for APC, 2% for PDP and no vote for others, voters with income 40-50k per month voted 5% for APC, 1% for PDP and no votes for others ,voters with income 50-60k per month voted 2% for APC, 2% for PDP and no vote for others , voters with income 60-80k per month voted 1% for APC, 2% for PDP and and 1% for others , voters with income 80-100k per month voted 5% for APC, 4% for PDP and no votes for others , voters with income 100+k per month voted 12% for APC, 8% for PDP and no vote for others, voters with no income voted 17% for APC, 14%for PDP and 1% for others

Voters in North-Central voted 9% for APC, 5% PDP, other Parties 1%, Voters in North-East voted 8% for APC, 4% PDP, other Parties 0%, Voters in North-West voted 21% for APC, 9% PDP, other Parties 0%, Voters in South-East voted 2% for APC, 6% PDP, other Parties 0%, Voters in South-South voted 8% for APC, 11% PDP, other Parties 1%, Voters in South-West voted 8% for APC, 6% PDP, other Parties 1%

Voters whose age-group range 18-25 years voted 8%APC , PDP 7% Other Parties 1%, voter age range 25-35 voted 28% APC,PDP 16% Other Parties 1%, voter age range 35-45 voted 14% APC,PDP 12% Other Parties 1%, voter age range 45-55 voted 6% APC,PDP 4% Other Parties 0%, voter age range 55+ voted 1% APC,PDP 1% Other Parties 0%

Opinion and Verdicts

Female voters are slightly likely to vote other parties than male voters. Voters age-groups 18-45 are more likely to vote other political parties compare to 45+ age-group. Voters in North-Central, South-West and South-South are more likely to vote others. Which means voters resentment against both APC and PDP are rife among women and in North-Central,South-West and South-South Geo-political zones and Age-Group 18-45 . Even though there is resentment , it is not enough to change the status quo of voters preferences among the leading political parties, APC and PDP which still hold 97% of voters preferences. Therefore our analysis was based on this 2 major parties. APC leads across all Geo-political zones except South-East and South-South and across all Age-Group, Income, Gender,and employment status.

On voters sentiments Buhari and Atiku has similar approval ratings. But APC have stronger approval rating than PDP, this is the reason why Buhari is leading Atiku. The polls result is 57% Buhari and 40% percent Atiku. The poll margin of error is plus or minus 3% which means true result of Buhari could be between 60% to 54% and Atiku is somewhere between 43%-37%. APC approval is 73% while PDP is 42%,if we average the party sentiments and candidate sentiment which is APC plus Buhari average rating (73+47)/2=60% ,which is similar to Buhari's higher margin of 60%, same for Atiku and PDP(47+42)=44.5% which is not too far from Atiku higher margin of 43%. Atiku is actually more popular than his party, his party is driving him down,while Buhari is lifted by his party.The Verdict is Buhari to win as of today, he is winning across all voters demography.

Polls Assumption

  • The sampling is based on 1000 participants between 21 October- 23 November 2018
  • The poll is based on social media participants, with voters who have PVC.
  • The sampling is based on exact turn out and voting weights per region in last elections
  • The margin of error is +-3% , meaning the result could be between +3% or -3% of actual results of polls.
  • Recent events might not be captured in this analysis but will be captured in the next polling results in December 2018
